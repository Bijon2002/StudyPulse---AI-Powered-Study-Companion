# 📚 How to Use Notebook AI - Quick Guide

## ⚠️ **IMPORTANT: Content Requirements**

The AI needs **proper study content** to work. Here's what you need:

### **Minimum Requirements:**

- ✅ **At least 20 words** (3-4 sentences)
- ✅ **Complete sentences** with periods
- ✅ **Actual study material** (not just names or single words)

### **❌ Won't Work:**

```
Title: "bijon"
Content: "bijon"
```

**Result**: "Content too short (1 word). Please add at least 20 words..."

### **✅ Will Work:**

```
Title: "Photosynthesis"
Content: "Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light
energy into chemical energy. It occurs in chloroplasts using chlorophyll.
The equation is 6CO2 + 6H2O + light → C6H12O6 + 6O2. This process is
essential for life on Earth."
```

**Result**: Full AI analysis with summaries, FAQs, flashcards, quizzes!

## 🎯 **Example Content to Try**

### **Example 1: Science (Photosynthesis)**

```
Title: Photosynthesis Basics
Subject: Biology
Content:
Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into
chemical energy. It occurs in chloroplasts, which contain chlorophyll that
captures sunlight. The process has two stages: light-dependent reactions
and the Calvin cycle. The overall equation is 6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy
→ C6H12O6 + 6O2. This means plants take in carbon dioxide and water, and
produce glucose and oxygen. Photosynthesis is crucial for life on Earth
as it produces the oxygen we breathe and forms the base of most food chains.
```

**What You'll Get:**

- ✅ Summary: First 3 sentences
- ✅ FAQs: 4 questions about photosynthesis
- ✅ Flashcards: 6 cards about key concepts
- ✅ Quiz: 3 multiple choice questions
- ✅ Mind Map: Connections between concepts
- ✅ Timeline: Step-by-step breakdown

### **Example 2: Math (Quadratic Equations)**

```
Title: Quadratic Equations
Subject: Mathematics
Content:
A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation of degree 2, written in the
form ax² + bx + c = 0, where a ≠ 0. The solutions can be found using the
quadratic formula: x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / 2a. The discriminant (b² - 4ac)
determines the nature of roots. If it's positive, there are two real roots.
If it's zero, there's one repeated root. If it's negative, there are two
complex roots. Quadratic equations can also be solved by factoring or
completing the square. They appear in many real-world applications like
projectile motion and area problems.
```

Python is a high-level, interpreted programming language known for its

### **Example 5: Chemistry (Periodic Table)**

```
Title: Periodic Table Organization
Subject: Chemistry
Content:
The periodic table organizes all known chemical elements based on their atomic
number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties. Elements are
arranged in rows called periods and columns called groups. Elements in the same
group have similar chemical properties because they have the same number of
valence electrons. The table is divided into metals, nonmetals, and metalloids.
Alkali metals in Group 1 are highly reactive, while noble gases in Group 18 are
very stable. The periodic table was first proposed by Dmitri Mendeleev in 1869.
Understanding the periodic table is fundamental to studying chemistry.
```

## 📝 **Step-by-Step Guide**

### **Step 1: Create Notebook**

1. Go to **Notebook** page
2. Click **"New Notebook"** button
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Title**: Short, descriptive name
   - **Subject**: Optional (e.g., "Biology", "Math")
   - **Content**: **IMPORTANT** - Add at least 20 words (3-4 sentences)

### **Step 2: Add Content**

Choose one of these options:

**Option A: Copy Example**

- Copy one of the examples above
- Paste into the Content field
- Click "Create Notebook"

**Option B: Write Your Own**

- Write at least 3-4 complete sentences
- Include key concepts and definitions
- Make sure it's about a study topic
- Click "Create Notebook"

**Option C: Upload File** (Coming soon)

- Click "Upload Sources"
- Select PDF, DOC, or TXT file
- Content will be extracted automatically

### **Step 3: Analyze**

1. Click on your notebook from the left sidebar
2. Wait 1.5 seconds for AI processing
3. View results in 3 tabs:
   - **Chat**: Summary + FAQs
   - **Studio**: Flashcards + Quiz + Mind Map
   - **Sources**: Original content

## 💡 **Tips for Best Results**

### **Do:**

- ✅ Write 3-5 complete sentences minimum
- ✅ Include key terms and definitions
- ✅ Use proper punctuation (periods, commas)
- ✅ Focus on one topic per notebook
- ✅ Add subject tags for organization

### **Don't:**

- ❌ Use single words or names only
- ❌ Write less than 20 words
- ❌ Skip punctuation
- ❌ Mix multiple unrelated topics
- ❌ Use only bullet points without sentences

## 🎯 **Common Issues & Solutions**

### **Issue 1: "Content too short"**

**Problem**: Entered only a few words
**Solution**: Add at least 3-4 complete sentences (20+ words)

### **Issue 2: "No flashcards generated"**

**Problem**: Content doesn't have clear keywords
**Solution**: Include specific terms and concepts in your content

### **Issue 3: "Quiz not working"**

**Problem**: Not enough content for questions
**Solution**: Add more detailed explanations and examples

### **Issue 4: "Summary shows warning"**

**Problem**: Content is too short or incomplete
**Solution**: Write complete sentences with proper punctuation
